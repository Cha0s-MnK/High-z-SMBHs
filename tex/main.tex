\begin{abstract}

This note summarizes the present implementation of the embedded intermediate-mass black hole (IMBH) treatment in the active Python version of the Gao+2023 globular-cluster (GC) model. The summary is restricted to behavior that is explicitly implemented in the current \texttt{src/} code. In the active pipeline, IMBH properties are assigned once at GC formation using a deterministic GC-scale seeding model in \texttt{src/IMBH.py}. The seeding step returns three birth-time quantities: a GC half-mass radius, a projected half-mass surface density, and an IMBH mass. These quantities are attached to each newly formed GC in \texttt{src/main\_spatial.py} and are then carried forward unchanged through the analytic disruption filter. No later IMBH update is present in the active \texttt{src/} evolution code.

\end{abstract}

\subsection{Scope of the present implementation}

In the present model, IMBH formation is treated as a birth property of each globular cluster. Every newly formed cluster is assigned a structural scale and an IMBH seed mass from its birth mass and metallicity. Thereafter the stellar cluster may lose mass through the adopted disruption prescription, but the IMBH seed itself is not further evolved. In particular, the current framework does not include later gas accretion, black-hole mergers, or any IMBH-dependent modification to the cluster orbit or tidal evolution.

\subsection{Cluster structural model at birth}

The seed prescription is anchored to a formation-time three-dimensional half-mass radius,
\begin{equation}
r_{\mathrm{h}}=
\frac{f_{\mathrm{h}}R_{4}}{1.3}
\left(\frac{M_{\mathrm{cl}}}{M_{\mathrm{pivot}}}\right)^{\beta},
\end{equation}
with the active default values listed in Table~\ref{tab:cfg}. Assuming a Plummer profile, this is converted to a projected half-mass radius,
\begin{equation}
R_{\mathrm{h,proj}}=\frac{r_{\mathrm{h}}}{1.305},
\end{equation}
and hence to the projected half-mass surface density
\begin{equation}
\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}=\frac{M_{\mathrm{cl}}}{2\pi R_{\mathrm{h,proj}}^{2}}.
\end{equation}
The seed model therefore depends on the cluster birth mass through both the explicit mass term and the induced variation in cluster compactness.

\subsection{Metallicity dependence and IMBH seed mass}

The cluster metallicity is written in the usual form
\begin{equation}
[\mathrm{Fe}/\mathrm{H}] = \log_{10}(Z/Z_{\odot}),
\end{equation}
so that
\begin{equation}
z \equiv Z/Z_{\odot} = 10^{[\mathrm{Fe}/\mathrm{H}]}.
\end{equation}
Before the IMBH fit is evaluated, the metallicity ratio is restricted to
\begin{equation}
10^{-2}\le z \le 1.
\end{equation}
Each newly formed GC receives an independent metallicity perturbation,
\begin{equation}
[\mathrm{Fe}/\mathrm{H}]_{\mathrm{GC}}=
[\mathrm{Fe}/\mathrm{H}]_{\mathrm{event}}+\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma_{m}),
\end{equation}
with \(\sigma_{m}=0.3\) dex, and this scattered metallicity is used for the seed calculation.

The IMBH mass itself is evaluated from metallicity-dependent fitting functions of \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}\). In the lower-density regime the model adopts
\begin{equation}
M_{\mathrm{IMBH},9}=
\begin{cases}
A(z)\log_{10}\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}+
B(z)\left[\log_{10}\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}\right]^{2}+C(z), &
\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}\ge \Sigma_{\mathrm{crit}}(z),\\
0, & \Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}<\Sigma_{\mathrm{crit}}(z),
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
where \(A\), \(B\), \(C\), and \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{crit}}\) are piecewise-linear functions of \(\log_{10}z\). In the high-density regime the continuation
\begin{equation}
M_{\mathrm{IMBH},10}=
\begin{cases}
D(z)\log_{10}\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}+E(z), &
\log_{10}\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}\ge 5.22,\\
0, & \log_{10}\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}<5.22
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
is used instead. The transition between the two branches is set by
\begin{equation}
M_{\mathrm{IMBH}}=
\begin{cases}
M_{\mathrm{IMBH},10}, & \log_{10}\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}\ge 5.22,\\
M_{\mathrm{IMBH},9}, & \log_{10}\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}<5.22.
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
Finally, seeds below
\begin{equation}
M_{\mathrm{IMBH,min}}=100\,M_{\odot}
\end{equation}
are discarded. The metallicity breakpoints entering the coefficient interpolation are \((0.126,\,0.398)\) for the first branch and \((0.079,\,0.316)\) for the second.

\subsection{Coupling to GC formation and later evolution}

The IMBH calculation is carried out once for every cluster formed in a GC-formation episode, including the most massive cluster in the event and all subsequent draws from the cluster initial mass function. The seeding step precedes the assignment of galactocentric radius, so the resulting IMBH mass depends only on the birth mass and metallicity of the cluster, not on its subsequent orbital placement within the host.

After formation, the stellar cluster mass is evolved through the adopted analytic disruption prescription. The IMBH-related quantities,
\begin{equation}
\left(r_{\mathrm{h}},\,\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}},\,M_{\mathrm{IMBH}}\right),
\end{equation}
are then carried forward unchanged. Thus the present model allows the stellar component of a GC to evolve while keeping the associated IMBH seed mass and its birth structural proxies fixed.

\subsection{Recorded quantities}

Both the full formation catalog and the corresponding survivor catalog retain the IMBH-related birth properties of each cluster. In practice, the stored quantities are the galactocentric radius assigned to the cluster, the half-mass radius \(r_{\mathrm{h}}\), the projected half-mass surface density \(\Sigma_{\mathrm{h}}\), and the IMBH seed mass. The survivor catalog name is historical; if the calculation is terminated at a redshift \(z_{\mathrm{final}}>0\), it still refers to objects surviving to that chosen final epoch rather than strictly to \(z=0\).
